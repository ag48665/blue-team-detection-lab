# Email Analysis

## Overview

The email claims that the recipient must confirm ownership of their email address.

The message attempts to create urgency and persuade the recipient to click a verification link.

---

## Observations

### Greeting

The email is addressed directly to the recipient:

> Dear brad

This makes the message appear more legitimate.

---

### Social Engineering

The email instructs the recipient to:

> Confirm your ownership

Attackers frequently use account verification themes to steal credentials.

---

### Suspicious Link

The visible button says:

> Confirm ownership

However, the hyperlink redirects to:

serverviro.com.co

instead of a legitimate Microsoft or corporate domain.

---

## Risk Assessment

High

The email contains characteristics commonly associated with phishing campaigns:

- Account verification request
- Embedded external hyperlink
- HTML formatting
- Social engineering language

---

## Verdict

Confirmed phishing email.
